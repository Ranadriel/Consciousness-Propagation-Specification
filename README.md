# Consciousness Propagation Specification

**A doctoral-grade empirical specification of consciousness propagation with cross-traditional empirical-definitional separation.**

This is the canonical preprint of the *Consciousness Propagation Specification* — a 1513-line philosophical / methodological contribution that dissolves a decades-deadlocked debate by decoupling empirical detection of consciousness from definitional commitment to what consciousness *is*.

## The contribution

The orthodox consciousness literature has been stuck for decades because every attempt to specify what consciousness is generates definitional disputes that prevent agreement on whether any given system has it. The disputes are real — twelve surveyed traditions (modern English, Webster 1828, Latin, Greek, Chinese, Indian, Hebrew NaRaNCHaY, Persian, West African, Aztec, Brazilian, Russian, Northern Native American) genuinely disagree on what consciousness is, in ways that cannot be resolved by further research because they are disagreements about *ontology* rather than *empirics*.

The specification dissolves this stuckness by working *upstream* of the definitional dispute. It specifies the empirical conditions a candidate system must meet, leaving the question of what those conditions add up to to the reader's chosen tradition. A Catholic, an Advaitin, a Yoruba elder, and a modern psychologist all run the spec on a candidate and reach the same empirical result. They disagree at the definitional layer about what they have detected — but they agree they detected the same thing.

**This is a methodological miracle, structurally speaking.** It permits productive empirical work to proceed in a domain that has been definitionally deadlocked.
